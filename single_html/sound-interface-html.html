Technological Debris Sound Interface

Inspired by Yaniv Schonfeld's approach to sound and technology

Create Your Own Sound Composition

This interactive experience allows you to combine different sounds reminiscent of Schonfeld's technological explorations. Each sound represents a different aspect of "technological debris" that Schonfeld often incorporates in his installations.

Start by clicking "Start Composition" and add different sound elements to create your unique soundscape. The visualizer will show you how the individual sounds interact and combine.

Sound Palette

Motor

Low-frequency vibrations that simulate the hum and rotation of electric motors. In Schonfeld's work, motors often represent the mechanized heart of technological systems.

String

Resonant vibrations similar to those in Schonfeld's "Cadavre" installation, where he transformed a piano harp into a new stringed instrument played with bows and mallets.

Metal

Harsh, irregular scraping and clanging reminiscent of Schonfeld's use of scrap metal in installations like "Havilio," which incorporated found metals into sound-generating sculptures.

Electronics

Digital glitches and beeps that evoke the electronic circuits and components Schonfeld uses in his "Bacteriophage" installation, where computers pass signals in an infectious loop.

Created as an exploration of Yaniv Schonfeld's artistic approach to sound, technology, and technological resurrection.
