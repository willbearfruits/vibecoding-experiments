Drone Metal Generator Code Example

// KillAllUnicorns Drone Metal Generator
// Example Python code for creating sustained drone tones

import numpy as np
from scipy.io import wavfile
import sounddevice as sd

class DroneGenerator:
    def __init__(self, base_freq=55.0, sample_rate=44100):
        self.base_freq = base_freq
        self.sample_rate = sample_rate
        self.feedback = 0.0
        
    def generate_drone(self, duration=10.0, distortion=0.8, overwhelm=0.5):
        """Generate a drone metal sound with increasing intensity."""
        samples = int(duration * self.sample_rate)
        t = np.linspace(0, duration, samples, False)
        
        # Generate base oscillators
        osc1 = np.sin(2 * np.pi * self.base_freq * t)
        osc2 = np.sin(2 * np.pi * (self.base_freq * 1.01) * t)  # Slight detuning
        osc3 = np.sin(2 * np.pi * (self.base_freq * 0.5) * t)   # Octave down
        
        # Combine oscillators
        signal = (osc1 + osc2 + osc3) / 3.0
        
        # Apply envelope for increasing intensity
        envelope = np.linspace(0.5, 1.0, samples) ** (1.0 - overwhelm)
        signal = signal * envelope
        
        # Apply distortion
        signal = np.tanh(signal * distortion * 10) / np.tanh(distortion * 10)
        
        # Add feedback/self-destruction
        for i in range(1, samples):
            if i > self.sample_rate:  # After 1 second
                feedback_idx = i - int(self.sample_rate * 0.1)  # 100ms delay
                self.feedback = self.feedback * 0.95 + signal[feedback_idx] * 0.05
                signal[i] += self.feedback * min(1.0, overwhelm * i / samples * 5)
        
        return signal
        
    def play(self, duration=30.0):
        """Generate and play a drone."""
        drone = self.generate_drone(duration)
        sd.play(drone, self.sample_rate)
        sd.wait()
        
    def save(self, filename, duration=30.0):
        """Generate and save a drone to a WAV file."""
        drone = self.generate_drone(duration)
        wavfile.write(filename, self.sample_rate, (drone * 32767).astype(np.int16))

# Create a drone generator
drone = DroneGenerator(base_freq=55.0)  # A1 note

# Generate and save a 30-second drone
drone.save("kill_all_unicorns_drone.wav", duration=30.0)

ABOUT THE VOID

KillAllUnicorns

KillAllUnicorns is one of the many aliases of Yaniv Schonfeld, an interdisciplinary artist working at the intersection of sound, technology, and performance. The brand emerged from years of experimental music projects, instrument building, and installations that explore the cycle of technological death and rebirth.

Each product is hand-crafted or personally designed, bringing together DIY electronics culture with conceptual art traditions. Our philosophy centers on creative destruction - finding new life in discarded technology and pushing the boundaries of what sound and music can be.

Nothing here is mass produced. Everything carries the traces of its creation, the mythology of its components, and the absurdity of its existence. Welcome to the void.

Myth & Technology

"In my work, I see technology as a modern mythology. The circuit becomes the labyrinth, the oscillator becomes the oracle, and the feedback loop becomes the Ouroboros - the snake eating its own tail.

When we resurrect dead technology, we participate in an ancient ritual dressed in contemporary garb. The synthesizer is our altar, the soldering iron our wand, and the noise we summon is our communion with forces beyond understanding."

- Yaniv Schonfeld

DIY Philosophy

We believe in demystifying technology through hands-on creation. Whether you're building one of our kits or using a finished instrument, you're encouraged to understand it, modify it, and make it truly yours. There are no sealed boxes here - only open systems waiting for your input.
